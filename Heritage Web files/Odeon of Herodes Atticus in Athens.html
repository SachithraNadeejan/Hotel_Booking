Odeon of Herodes Atticus in Athens

The Odeon of Herodes Atticus is a stone theater structure located on the southwest slope of the Acropolis of Athens. It was built in 161 AD by the Roman philosopher Herodes Atticus in memory of his wife Aspasia Annia Regilla.

Immerse yourself in the timeless beauty and cultural significance of this ancient amphitheater, surrounded by the rich history of Athens.
